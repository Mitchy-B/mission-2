Dive Locations

Aotearoa is a wonderful country for diving, due to its wide variety of sea-life, artificial reefs and natural beautiful locations.


Mathesons Bay

Located an hour and a half north of Auckland CBD in the small coastal town of Leigh, this dive location has A large rock Island and smaller rocky formations just a short swim out off the shore, teaming with Snapper, Wrasse, Kina, Rays, and has even been known to have Orcas chase Rays right into the bay.

Goat Island

Not far from Mathesons bay, we have the Goat Island Marine Reserve. Because the area is protected, the marine life flourishes. Prepare to see some big nosy Snapper, as well as some big Crayfish! But remember, as delicious as they look, the are is protected, so leave nothing but bubbles.

HMNZS Canterbury

THe HMNZS Canterbury is a decommissioned NZ Navy Frigate, scuttled in Deep Water Cove as an artificial reef, to help encourage depleted fish stocks to increase. It is easiest to access by boat, with Te Uenga Bay being the closest place to launch from, and is a sandy beach. Otherwise Paihia is about 16km away by boat. There is a Rahui in place to protect fish stocks, and this makes for cool sea life to be seen around and throughout the ship. Because of it being in a sheltered cove, the ship is well preserved and is easily one of my favourite dive spots.

Training

Wanting to learn to dive? A bit rusty and want to sharpen your skills?

There are many good dive shops around the country where you can go to learn to dive, however the team at Auckland Scuba are extremely knowledgeable, friendly, and located just over in the North Shore of Auckland, in Rosedale.

Dive Equipment

Need to hire or buy dive gear?

The team at Auckland Scuba have everything you need to get you ready for your underwater adventures. Visit them at their Rosedale store, and they will help you find the best equipment suited for your needs. Quality gear can be expensive, and it can take a while to accumulate all of your own stuff.
